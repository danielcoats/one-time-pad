One-Time Pad An online demonstration

The One-Time Pad is a method of encryption and the only known perfect cipher. If used correctly, a message encrypted using the One-Time Pad is impossible to crack.

One-Time Pad

Rules

  • The key must be at least as long as the message that you want to send.
  • Each key must be used only once, and must be destroyed after use.
  • Only the sender and receiver may have a copy of the key.
  • The key must be truly random.

How it works

Enciphering

  1. Take your secret message. For example, SECRETCODE.
  2. Assign a number to each letter of the alphabet. For example, A = 0, B = 1 … Z = 25.
  3. Turn your secret message into digits using the mapping above. For example, 18 04 02 17 04 19 02 14 03 04.
  4. Create your random key, which is at least as long as your message, for example EQUHESEVZO, and convert it to the digits 04 16 20 07 04 18 04 21 25 14.
  5. Now add each letter in the message to the corresponding digit in the key, mod 26. For example, S + E mod 26 = 22 mod 26 = 22 = W or O + V mod 26 = 14 + 21 mod 26 = 35 mod 26 = 9 = J. Enciphering SECRETCODE with the key EQUHESEVZO gives WUWYILGJCS (the ciphertext).
  6. Send the cipher, and the cipher only, to your friend, who must already know the secret key.

Deciphering

  1. Take the ciphertext WUWYILGJCS that you have received and convert it into digits as above, which gives 22 20 22 24 08 11 06 09 02 18.
  2. Now subtract each letter in the secret key from the ciphertext. For example, W - E mod 26 = 22 - 4 mod 26 = 18 = S.
  3. And there you have it, the secret message: SECRETCODE. Now make sure to destroy your secret key.
